## Abstract

This document proposes the Knowledge Compounding Theory (KCT), a conceptual framework for understanding how learning investments generate value over time.

KCT argues that knowledge assets do not age equally. Some depreciate, some persist, and some compound.

The theory introduces Experience Extraction, Durable Concepts, Knowledge Networks, Domain Transferability, and AI Amplification as mechanisms for understanding long-term capability formation.

## Origins

KCT emerged from a simple question:

**What survived forty years of learning?**

Many technologies disappeared. Many frameworks became obsolete. Yet certain concepts continued generating value across decades and domains.

## Core Principle

Knowledge does not age equally.

The objective of learning is not merely to accumulate knowledge.

The objective of learning is to extract durable concepts from experiences.

## Knowledge Network Effects

Durable concepts gain value through connectivity.

Concepts rarely compound in isolation.

## Knowledge Compounding Theory

Knowledge compounds when it exhibits:

1. Domain Applicability
2. Time Persistence
3. Reusability
4. Learning Amplification
5. Conceptual Connectivity
6. Transferability

## Knowledge Compounding Index

KCI = Domain Applicability × Time Persistence × Reusability

A conceptual heuristic, not a validated scientific measure.

## Domain Transferability

Durable concepts derive value from their ability to transfer across domains.

## Mathematics as a Universal Amplifier

Mathematics repeatedly appears across science, engineering, economics, optimization, and AI.

Its value derives from its ability to amplify future learning.

## AI Amplification Hypothesis

AI may increase the returns of compounding concepts by enabling faster traversal of conceptually connected knowledge networks.
