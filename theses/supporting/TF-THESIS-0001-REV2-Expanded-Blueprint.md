# Abstract

This publication argues that computing is evolving toward an Execution Economy, where governed participants exchange execution as a first-class economic activity. While Artificial Intelligence reduces the cost of generating intelligence, sustainable economic value emerges only when intelligence is translated into trusted, governed, accountable execution.

This thesis positions the existing TextFind and PER architecture, together with the RFC portfolio, as an exploration of the infrastructure primitives required to support such an economy.

# Chapter 1 — Introduction

Throughout history, economic systems expanded by increasing the scale at which assets could be exchanged.

Roads enabled physical goods exchange.

Telecommunications enabled information exchange.

The Internet enabled global digital exchange.

Artificial Intelligence enables the large-scale production of intelligence.

Yet intelligence itself is not the final asset.

Organizations derive value from execution.

The central claim of this thesis is:

> The next major economic abstraction is governed execution.

# Chapter 5 — The Limits of Context Engineering

Context Engineering is an important advancement.

It improves:

- Memory
- Relevance
- Retrieval
- Personalization

However, context alone cannot answer:

- Should this action be allowed?
- Who authorized it?
- Can it be delegated?
- Can the outcome be trusted?

Context improves intelligence.

Governance improves execution.
